Michelle Quan | Living In Harmony




In 1978, Chinese-Vietnmaese immigrant Tan Lu and his family immigrated from Vietnam to America in search of a better life. After years of trying to gain financial stability, in 1983 Lu invested his savings into a small old building and renovated it into a small restaurant on William Street, Downtown San Jose and named it TK Noodles #1. Although unassuming on the outside, they worked hard to build a loyal customer base with delicious, but affordable chinese-styled noodles. Due to the success of the original, some of Lu's former business partners and family members copied Lu's business formula from the name to the menu and interior design, naming themselves as New Tung Kee Noodles. In addition, the original copy is also situated in Downtown San Jose albeit on Santa Clara Street. Rather than sue the copy, both companies decided to work on non-speaking terms and grow on their own terms.

To convey the similarity of the businesses living and growing in harmony as well as the popularity with the Bay Area Asian population, I based my stamp and sign off the Yin Yang Symbol. The Yin Yang symbol historically represents balance and harmony, especially in Chinese culture. With the sign, I used red and yellow based on the original colors of both business's logos.
